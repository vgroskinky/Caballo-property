The Full Picture

A complete recreational compound in the shadow of the Caballo Mountains

Set on 1.66 acres in Seco Creek Estates, this turnkey compound was assembled in 2023 with intention: a well-appointed manufactured home, a serious insulated heated workshop, a 30×30 steel garage barn, and an open-air RV shelter — all fully electrified, all on a private well and septic, all facing the dramatic Caballo and Black Range mountains.

Ride ATVs directly from the property onto miles of trails across public land. One mile from the front door, paved road gives way to a public land dirt road leading to the Rio Grande — fishing, small boat access, cottonwoods, and the mountains rising beyond. When the river runs low, cross and push deeper into the Caballos. No trailhead. No trailer. The trails begin at your gate.

This stretch of the Rio Grande is a designated stop on the Southwest New Mexico Birding Trail — the Caballo Christmas Bird Count regularly records the highest species total in New Mexico. Within 12 miles, the GPAA Greyback gold prospecting claims draw members from across the country. The Sierra County geology is genuine.

The owners purchased the land outright, then added the home, garage, workshop, and RV shelter — approximately $350,000 in improvements on land they owned free and clear. Life intervened, and they are selling well below replacement cost. The right buyer gets a rare thing: a complete, move-in-ready rural compound in a location that takes years to fully appreciate.

The Setting

Southern New Mexico at its most elemental

Caballo sits in the Rio Grande rift valley between two mountain ranges — the Caballos to the east, the Black Range to the west. The light changes by the hour. Summers bring dramatic thunderstorms building over the peaks. Winters dust the ridgelines in snow while the valley floor stays mild.

This is bird country, fishing country, prospecting country, ATV country, and wide-sky country. A designated stop on the Southwest New Mexico Birding Trail, the Rio Grande corridor below Caballo hosts the highest Christmas Bird Count species total in New Mexico — sandhill cranes, raptors, warblers, kingfishers filling the cottonwoods on any given day. Caballo Lake State Park is 20 minutes away. The GPAA Greyback gold prospecting claims are 12 miles away — Sierra County's geology has drawn serious prospectors for generations. Miles of ATV trails run directly from the property across public land, with river crossings into the Caballos when conditions allow. Truth or Consequences — with restaurants, hot springs, a free public shooting range, and full services — is 15 miles north on NM-187.

The Seco Court cul-de-sac is quiet. The neighbors are few. The stars at night are genuinely extraordinary.

On the Water

Two world-class lakes. Both within reach.

Sierra County holds one of the great open secrets in southwestern recreation. Caballo Lake is 20 minutes from your front door — bass, walleye, catfish, and crappie in the shadow of the mountains that give the lake its name. Thirty minutes north, Elephant Butte Lake is the largest body of water in New Mexico — 40 miles long, over 200 miles of shoreline, and a national reputation for record-breaking striped bass and largemouth. The 30×30 garage fits your boat and trailer without compromise. The RV shelter handles the tow rig. You don't store your boat at a marina — you store it at home.

On the Trails

The Caballo Mountains. Out your front gate.

The Caballo Mountains are one of the premier off-road destinations in southern New Mexico. Trails run from easy desert two-tracks to technical rock climbs rated up to 10 on the Chile Challenge scale — the largest off-road event in southern New Mexico, held annually in the Caballos for over 30 years. Palomas Gap, Backstage Pass, Turtleback — named trails that draw Jeep and UTV enthusiasts from across the Southwest. From this property, the trailhead is your gate.
